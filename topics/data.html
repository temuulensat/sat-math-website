Lesson 1: Ratios & Proportions

Goal: Use ratios to compare quantities and solve proportions.

Key ideas:

  • A ratio compares two quantities: 3:2 means 3 parts to 2 parts.
  • A proportion is an equation: a/b = c/d.
  • Cross-multiply to solve: a·d = b·c.

Example 1: Flour:sugar = 5:2. If flour is 20 cups, sugar is how many cups?

  • Scale factor = 20 ÷ 5 = 4
  • Sugar = 2 × 4 = 8 cups

Example 2: If x/12 = 5/8, find x.

  • Cross-multiply: 8x = 12·5 = 60
  • x = 60/8 = 7.5

Common mistakes:

  • Flipping only one side of a proportion.
  • Mixing units (miles vs. hours).

Quick Practice:

  1. Paint:water = 3:1. If paint is 15, water is?
  2. y/9 = 4/3. Find y.

Answer Key: (1) 5 (2) 12

Lesson 2: Percent & Unit Rates

Goal: Solve problems involving percent change and “per 1” rates.

Percent basics:

  • Percent means “out of 100.” 25% = 25/100 = 0.25
  • Percent change = (new − old) / old

Example 1 (Percent increase): Price goes from 50 to 60.

  • Change = 10
  • Percent increase = 10/50 = 0.2 = 20%

Example 2 (Unit rate): 150 miles in 2.5 hours.

  • Rate = 150 / 2.5 = 60 miles per hour

Quick Practice:

  1. Decrease from 80 to 68. Percent decrease?
  2. 24 dollars for 6 items. Cost per item?

Answer Key: (1) 15% (2) $4

Lesson 3: Statistics (Mean/Median)

Goal: Understand averages and how data is summarized.

Key definitions:

  • Mean: sum ÷ count
  • Median: middle value when sorted
  • Range: max − min

Example 1: Mean of 4 numbers is 12. What is the sum?

  • Sum = 12 × 4 = 48

Example 2: Data: 3, 7, 7, 10, 21. Median?

  • Already sorted, middle is 7

Quick Practice:

  1. Mean of 5 numbers is 8. Sum?
  2. Median of 2, 5, 9, 12 is?

Answer Key: (1) 40 (2) (5+9)/2 = 7
