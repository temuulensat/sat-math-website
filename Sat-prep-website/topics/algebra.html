Lesson 1: Linear Equations

Goal: Solve for x by isolating the variable using inverse operations.

Steps:

  • Combine like terms on each side if needed.
  • Move constants (numbers) to the other side using +/−.
  • Move x-terms to one side.
  • Divide/multiply to make the coefficient of x equal to 1.
  • Quick check: plug your answer back into the original equation.

Example 1: 2x + 6 = 14

  • Subtract 6 from both sides: 2x = 8
  • Divide by 2: x = 4
  • Check: 2(4)+6 = 14 ✅

Example 2: 5x − 3 = 2x + 12

  • Subtract 2x from both sides: 3x − 3 = 12
  • Add 3 to both sides: 3x = 15
  • Divide by 3: x = 5
  • Check: 5(5)−3 = 22 and 2(5)+12 = 22 ✅

Common mistakes:

  • Forgetting to do the same operation to both sides.
  • Sign errors (subtracting a negative incorrectly).

Quick Practice:

  1. 3x + 7 = 22
  2. 4x − 9 = 11
  3. 6x + 5 = 2x + 25

Answer Key: (1) x=5, (2) x=5, (3) x=5

Lesson 2: Systems of Equations

Goal: Find the point (x, y) that makes both equations true.

Two main methods:

  • Substitution: Solve one equation for a variable, then plug into the other.
  • Elimination: Add/subtract equations to cancel one variable.

Example 1 (Substitution):

y = 2x + 1 and y = -x + 7

  • Set equal: 2x + 1 = -x + 7
  • Add x: 3x + 1 = 7
  • Subtract 1: 3x = 6 → x = 2
  • Plug in: y = 2(2) + 1 = 5
  • Solution: (2, 5)

Example 2 (Elimination):

x + y = 9 and x - y = 1

  • Add equations: 2x = 10
  • x = 5
  • Plug in: 5 + y = 9 → y = 4
  • Solution: (5, 4)

Common mistakes:

  • Forgetting to multiply an equation when using elimination.
  • Solving for x but not finding y.

Quick Practice:

  1. y = x + 3 and y = 2x - 1
  2. 2x + y = 11 and x - y = 1

Answer Key: (1) (4,7) (2) (4,3)

Lesson 3: Word Problems

Goal: Translate real-world situations into algebraic equations and solve them.

Strategy:

  • Define a variable.
  • Turn words into math.
  • Write an equation.
  • Solve and check.

Example 1:

A taxi charges $4 plus $1.50 per mile. If the ride costs $19, how many miles were traveled?

  • Let m = miles.
  • 4 + 1.5m = 19
  • 1.5m = 15
  • m = 10

Example 2:

A number increased by 7 is equal to 3 times the number. What is the number?

  • Let x = number.
  • x + 7 = 3x
  • 7 = 2x
  • x = 3.5

Common mistakes:

  • Not defining the variable.
  • Mixing up “more than” vs “times”.

Quick Practice:

  1. A gym charges $25 plus $10 per month. Total cost is $95. How many months?
  2. Twice a number minus 5 equals 9. Find the number.

Answer Key: (1) 7 months (2) 7
